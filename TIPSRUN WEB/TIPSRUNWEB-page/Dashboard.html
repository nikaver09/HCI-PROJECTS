TIPS AFTER RUN

Tips for recovery after a physical run or workout?

Cool Down

  • Walk for 5–10 minutes to gradually lower your heart rate and prevent blood pooling.

    • Hydrate

  • Replace lost fluids with water or an electrolyte drink, especially after long or sweaty runs.

    • Stretch Lightly

  • Focus on dynamic stretches or gentle static stretches for major muscles: calves, quads, hamstrings, and hip flexors.

    • Within 1–2 Hours

  • Include carbs to replenish glycogen and protein to repair muscle tissue (ideal ratio is about 3:1 carbs to protein).
  • Examples: smoothie with fruit and protein powder, chicken and rice, Greek yogurt with granola.

    • Foam Roll

  • Use a foam roller or massage gun to release tight spots and improve blood flow.

    • Later in the Day / Evening

    Take a Nap or Rest

  • Even 20–30 minutes of rest or sleep helps repair muscle tissue.

    • Contrast Therapy (Optional)

  • Some runners alternate hot and cold showers or use an ice bath to reduce inflammation.

    • Stay Mobile

  • Don’t stay seated all day. Light walking or gentle yoga helps prevent stiffness.

    • Next Day and Beyond

    Sleep Well

  • Aim for 7–9 hours to support muscle repair and immune function.

    • Listen to Your Body

  • It’s okay to take a rest or active recovery day (easy cycling, swimming, or yoga) if you're sore or fatigued.
